What is Allegra AI?

In today's interconnected world, learning a language is in higher demand than ever, and there's no better platform to improve one's language skills than with Allegra AI. Allegra AI allows the user to upload their favorite songs in their target language, and answer practice questions regarding the grammatical structure and vocabulary of the lyrics. With each task, the app reinforces the user's understanding of the song's meaning and clear enunciation, improving proficiency. Then, using a page ranking algorithm, the application ranks users against each other to compete for a top spot.

What sets Allegra AI apart is its innovative use of a page ranking algorithm to gamify the learning process. By ranking users against each other based on their performance and progress, the app creates a competitive yet supportive environment that motivates users to strive for excellence. Whether aiming to claim the top spot or simply looking to challenge oneself, users find themselves continually engaged and driven to improve their language skills.

Who is Aylin Ambriz?


I am Aylin Ambriz, creator of Allegra AI and a freshman at UC Berkeley studying Statistics and Data Science. My inspiration for this project stemmed from my previous language learning experiences, where translating my favorite songs served as a pivot for my language journey. I never had a centralized platform that allowed me to interact with a song's lyrics for learning purposes, which is why I created Allegra AI.

My language journey started when my dad handed me his French dictionary in third grade and told me to "have fun." Supplemented by the French lessons I took in elementary and the Spanish I already spoke at home, I picked up French through natural acquisition--until I forgot about half of it.

My passion for languages was reignited by a foreign song I stumbled upon Youtube when I was 12. The song was so addicting--I played it numerous times, and yet I didn't understand what language it was sung in. From there, I committed to learning the language to understand the artist and their music. Two years later, I reached fluency in Portuguese. Currently I am learning Japanese and Chinese (and relearning French), which I have been continuously studying for 4 years.

In the future, I hope to scale this project to allow users to choose languages besides Spanish to learn from, thereby allowing for a more comprehensive user experience.
